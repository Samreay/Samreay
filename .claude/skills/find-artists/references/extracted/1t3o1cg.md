## Post body

Today brings you the third book in the Dao of Healing series, though unfortunately the audiobook is not yet ready for your listening pleasure and will be out in a few months! To give you a taste of this new chapter of the story, the blurb:

*Zhao Dan returns to his sect to find a conflict brewing amongst his disciples. When one of them almost dies, he realises he must take a firmer hand in leading them. By organising a tournament in the Grasping Life Sect, he hopes to give the disciples a better way of battling it out. Within the tournaments, healing experts and martial demons emerge, while outside the sect the political situation turns complex and potentially deadly.*

*After resolving issues at home, another threat rears its head as the next step of the Age of Ascension emerges in the form of the Black River Gate. Zhao Dan, Wang Ren, and a number of allies must hunt down elusive keys in order to access the grand secret realm of the Black River, where even more dangers and untold treasures await them.*

The first two books have been on amazon for a while now and I am glad that a fresh audience was able to enjoy the story after its fantastic run on RoyalRoad. For those unfamiliar with the series, it features a war doctor who died and was reincarnated in a world of cultivation and chaos; where he must use the talents of his old life to find purpose in the new. The blurb of the first book is included below if you are new here, as are the links to all the released editions.

# Book 1 Blurb

Zhao Dan was born with a hopeless fate. Untalented, poor; a worthless outer sect disciple with no future worth speaking of.

Then I woke up in his body, moments after his suicide.

This strange new world was filled with bloodthirsty beasts, and cultivators who were worse. There was only one thing I could do to stay sane, and it was what I did best—being a doctor.  
After a spiritually defining oath to the heavens, and a near-death encounter with a serpentine spirit beast, I acquired the tools to do exactly that.

And the first problem reared its ugly head even sooner than I’d expected: a corrupt city lord and his insidious advisor.  
Even after curing the ills that plagued Three River City, I discovered that the world itself was damaged beyond repair. Luckily, I knew a pretty great doctor…

Inspired by Laws of Cultivation, Ancient Godly Monarch, and The Wrong Way to use Healing Magic
