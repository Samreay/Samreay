## Post body

Imagine a world where the old myths didn't just fade away—they were overthrown.

After Prometheus rose in rebellion against the heavens, the old order was shattered, and "New Gods" forged a rigid, magical system to govern the remnants of the world. This is the world where Emilia is reborn.

Starting from her very first breath, Emilia’s journey is one of survival against the crushing weight of poverty and a harsh social hierarchy. There are no shortcuts or "chosen one" prophecies here—only the slow, meticulous mastery of herbalism, the dangerous art of talisman crafting, and the struggle to find enough resources to fuel her growth as a mage.

**What to expect from the story:**

* **A Mythological Reimagining:** A deep dive into a world shaped by the fallout of a divine revolution. The world-building explores how a society functions when its very laws are dictated by a celestial "System" born of conflict.
* **True Coming-of-Age:** We follow Emilia from birth through her formative years. It’s a granular look at how a child with a grown woman's mind navigates a dangerous new reality.
* **Hard Magic & Crafting:** If you enjoy stories where magic feels like a science—requiring study, rare minerals, and precise techniques—this focus on crafting and formations will resonate with you.
* **High Stakes & Personal Growth:** Beyond the magic, this is a story about a girl starting from nothing and aiming for the stars in a world that wasn't built for her to succeed.

### Comment odcvzy7 (parent t1_odcego0)

I didn't have a massive grand plan at the start. I was just tired of OP characters with cheat skills and wanted to write a progression fantasy that felt earned. The idea of an isekai protagonist reborn as a baby seemed like it offered the best opportunities for that. I mostly just wrote the tropes I enjoy, while keeping in mind what usually works for readers in the genre.

I’ve always liked crafting, so that was a natural choice. To be honest, seeing Emilia win a fight over 10 silver coins with a merchant - as a poor kid from the slums - feels like a much stronger moment to me than the repetitive battles you see in every other story. It’s not that there’s no action, because there's plenty of it and I try to keep a good balance, but books that focus almost entirely on combat tend to get boring for me pretty quickly.
