## Post body

First of all, look at that damn cover. It's gorgeous!

I can't lie, it's my favorite in the series.

With this release, books 1, 2, and 3 are now out on Amazon in Kindle (Unlimited), Audible (by Johnathan McClain), and Paperback (Though I hope you read it, it looks great on a coffee table).

**Quick blurb**: *In Strandbinder, Walker Reed ascends to a new level of power just as Symphony begins to outgrow his control. With souls, worlds, and civilizations now hanging in the balance of his choices, Walker must face the truth of who he is and what he's willing to do to protect everything he's ever built. Because in a universe ruled by evolution, creation itself may be the deadliest power of all.*

**Non-generic blurb of what to expect:**
*-System Creation*
*-Loads of humor*
*-Exploration of power and what it means to be a small-g god*
*-The building of a LitRPG world from scratch, including skills*
*-Great(er) power with great(er) consequences*

I like to equate LitRPG stories to video game genres. For instance, QuestWright, my current series, is similar to a good ole-fashioned RTS. My next series, Support Your Local Warmage, would be more along the lines of Red Dead Redemption 2 mixed with Prison Architect, just less killing.

For Symphony, on the other hand:
Book 1: ARK + RimWorld + Elden Ring progression energy
Book 2: RimWorld + Frostpunk + Black & White
Book 3: Civilization + Divinity: Original Sin + MadGames Tycoon 2

I'm a huge Civilization nut, so this is definitely my favorite of the first three. There's only one more book to come after, so I hope you'll take a look. And, if you do, know that you've made an old(er) guy really happy.
