>Then a message appeared in his vision:  
>  
>**60,000 year countdown has ended**  
>  
>**Nemesis 1 released**  
>  
>**Please acknowledge**

>  
>And with the dawn of the Void, he'll become the most important person to have ever lived.

US link: [https://www.amazon.com/Dawn-Void-Book/dp/B0BY3GPJXS/](https://www.amazon.com/Dawn-Void-Book/dp/B0BY3GPJXS/)

Here's what some fellow authors said about the book:

* "I've read post-apocalyptic books before, but nothing like this one. This one is different. This one cuts deep, the tension always feels real, the stakes monumental, and the characters on the ragged edge. This book is simply fantastic." *- Davis Ashura, bestselling author of Testament of Steel*
* "The Dawn of the Void is a brilliantly conceived, adrenaline-pumping ride with a wonderful balance of science fiction, fantasy, and military fantasy. I could not put it down and can't wait to see where the story goes next." *- Dyrk Ashton, bestselling author of The Paternus Trilogy*
* "Apocalypses are a dime a dozen, but most stories either try to stop them or just rush through to play around in the post-apocalyptic wasteland. Not Dawn of the Void. Phil Tucker set out to explore the inside of the apocalypse- the collapse of our systems, the selfishness of the wealthy and powerful, and the courage of regular people stuck in hell. Not the easiest goal, but one Tucker scored with aplomb. Dawn of the Void turns the apocalypse into one badass roller coaster ride." *- John Bierce, bestselling author of The Mage Errant series.*

If you're in the mood for a gritty, deep dive into how regular folks would rise to the occasion as all hell breaks loose across the world, then give this series a shot. I'm really proud of this trilogy, and had a blast exploring how everyone - from governments to the military to regular folks  - would react to demons spilling out of random portals with a singular intent: to kill.

Cheers!
