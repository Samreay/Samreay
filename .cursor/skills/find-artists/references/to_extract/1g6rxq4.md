**Blurb**:

Riza’s quest to outgrow the dangers of her new world never ends. Assigned to hunt demons in the giant worm-infested tunnels beneath the Bread Basket, she and her companions, Jakks and Lefie, face a deadly scourge deep below the earth.

When a sudden collapse traps them underground, Riza’s paranoia takes hold. With dwindling supplies and enemies lurking in the shadows, she must push her abilities beyond their limits, breaking down the very mechanics of magic in a desperate bid for freedom.

But such power demands sacrifice, and as Riza navigates the labyrinthine tunnels, she uncovers unsettling truths about the demons that stalk her, the Ancients whose echoes linger in the darkness, and the perilous cost of her relentless drive to survive.



**Series Info**:

**Narration Style**: Primarily first person past tense with infrequent shifts to other characters, written in third person.

**Protagonist's Abilities**: She's a necromancer, which means her weaknesses involve anything physical and one-on-one fights. These weaknesses become pronounced in book 2, when she finally starts fighting stronger enemies. She focuses on magic, primarily on the Life skill tree, which includes skills like Heal, but there's also a large emphasis on elemental magic, like Water, with her companions.

She's not built to be a fighter, remaining as fragile as a normal human regardless of her level, so she relies on magical experimentation, scouting, and her necromantic minions, as well as her companions, for combat.

**Progression Methods**: Standard litRPG stuff; killing monsters grants levels which grant stat points and skill points. There's a level cap which can be increased by killing stronger monsters. This forces Riza to make the most out of every skill since there's a no guarantee she will get more. A large portion of the series focuses on wringing every bit of strength and utility out of her skills, even if they're explicitly non-combat skills, through ingenuity and scientific knowledge. Expect deep exploration of the mechanics of magic.

**Progression Speed**: Slow. The world, for the most part, is safe. Riza is a normal person who doesn't want to throw herself into life threatening situations. Levels are hard to come by, but each level presents a larger and larger increase in power. The majority of Riza's strength comes from experimentation and preparation. Knowledge is Riza's greatest weapon.

**Magic System Style**: Extremely hard and crunchy. There's often a physical and mathematical basis for the magic, and knowledge of science directly translates into being better able to use and exploit magic. Combined with the mathematical basis for skills, it's possible to plan out builds far in advance.

**Tone:** Realistic. It can be dark, because throwing an ordinary girl into an isekai situation is horrifying, but Riza is a tough cookie and there's moments of levity as well. The world and situation are taken seriously and Riza never loses hope.
