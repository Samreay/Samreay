Overpowered Dungeon Boy, Book Two drops today, completing the mini-series and my first ever published story (book one dropped in May).

https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0CD1JJHS2

>“A romping fast-paced fantasy adventure with a ridiculously overpowered main character in the trope of Shrek and One Punch Man.”

Thomas is born in a monster dungeon and fights for his life daily, along with his pet, Gina. When he finally escapes the dungeon, he's overpowered and life outside is very different from what he expected. 

It’s a fun, relatively short read at 350 pages and book 1 has 500+ ratings, averaging 4.7/5, so I hope you check out the story! My wife says book two is way better than book one, so there's that too!

I leaned hard into the one-punch man trope, so there are plenty of scenes where the MC is underestimated and snubbed before blowing away expectations. He's a great fighter but clueless about several other things. 

Is it progression fantasy? 
The main character gains a couple of new abilities in each book (plus several side characters either level up or develop skills). But be aware the progression is light and entirely ancillary to the story. Could the MC of the second book defeat the MC of the first book? *Absolutely*. But this story is less about the MC’s growth and more about the interplay between his cluelessness, his supreme power dominance, and people continually underestimating him (again, think One Punch Man).
