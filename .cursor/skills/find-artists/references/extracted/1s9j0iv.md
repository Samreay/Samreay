## Post body

If you know the signs, you can already tell. But I've signed with Mango Media Publishing and MAZE will soon be an E-book on Amazon! **Specifically on May 21st.**

This also means that you have until **May 20th** to read book 1 on Royal Road before it gets stubbed.

I've been writing this story for over six years already, the original version spans more than *1000 chapters* and it's not yet finished, as I'm still actively writing more. And I will say it is a slow burn story.

If you expect characters to be completely different by the end of book one, that's not going to happen. But they grow, they become more powerful, and little by little they become someone who's very different from who they started the story as, while still being the same character.

Let me tell you my *~~very biased~~* opinion on why this story is great.

The story at its core is about two things, and the most important one is a **dream**.

**Hera**, our MC. She's just a girl who had a dream, but things didn't workout. Until by luck and circumstance she gains a second shot at her dream. It is something that I think a lot of people can relate.

>Maybe you had a dream career, but for various reasons, weren't able to go down that path. Maybe you wanted to be a singer, an actor, an astronaut, yet it wasn't in the cards for you.

Now, what if someone offered to stack the deck for you? What if someone opened a door and showed you a way to reach that dream?

That's exactly what happens to Hera. She still very much has to do the work, and there are no tangible promises that everything is going to happen as she wants, but at least she can try.

And the story is about Hera following her dreams.

What dream is that? To be an explorer. In this world, there is a place called **MAZE**. Scientists say this is an acronym for **M**ulti-dimensional **A**nomaly **Z**one **E**verlasting. But that's just a way for them to have a proper name.

The maze is an infinite labyrinth of different biomes called Rooms. Think of it like zones in an old school MMO. And they can be anything.

* A gigantic 400km² cave with cities, forests, and a river connecting to the ocean.
* An archipelago that was once inhabited by dragons.
* A hyper futuristic moon base. A field of charred rocks surrounded by rivers of magma with a gigantic city in the center of a caldera.
* An ancient forest with ruins from a civilization that lived so long ago that they make the Pyramids look like modern structures.
* A Victorian styled clockwork city where the buildings literally grow from the ground like trees.
* One long ass aqueduct being used as a road.
* A city entirely built inside a mountain.

Be honest, knowing you have access to places like these and infinite more, kind of makes you understand why this was her dream. Oh, and did I mention there are also **dungeons**? Like MMO dungeons. If you think the Rooms are wild, just wait. Rooms still follow rules, still have a pattern. In a dungeon, all that goes out of the window

A bee war, a mushroom rave, the valley of poison, a monster hunter style island, a battle between Kobold (lizard) and Kobold (wolf) to decide who has the right to that name. Dungeons that replicate ancient events, dungeons that serve just to fish, dungeons that ask you to run away from an unkillable and unstoppable creature. Dungeons that are the equivalent of a real life mario party game.

***They are all there.***

The story follows Hera as she explores go on adventures. She meets new people, discovers some secrets, and encounters some not so human friends who can share more about the MAZE.
