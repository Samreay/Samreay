Hi everyone! You've probably seen me around, recommending Cradle, re-reading Cradle, dreaming about Cradle. You know... the norm.

But now I'm here to tell you **not** to read Cradle. Nor even listen to it! Listen to my book instead!

## [Audible Link](https://www.audible.com/pd/Scion-of-Storms-Audiobook/B0C8ZM4HD6) ... [YouTube Link](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dQw4w9WgXcQ) ... [Epub link anyway](https://www.amazon.com/Scion-Storms-Manifestation-Cultivation-Novel-ebook/dp/B0BYBYD826)

If you haven't listened to book one yet, I'd probably [recommend starting there](https://www.audible.com/pd/Soul-Relic-Audiobook/B0BTZTNQZM), its a slow-burn travel-heavy arc about Raysha's quest to fix her lack of ability to regenerate aeon. But if you have read or listened to it, here's the blurb for book two:

******

**It's time for Raysha to embrace her power.**

After an arduous journey to integrate an ancient spirit heart into her core, Raysha sets out to prove her worth in the Academy's tournament.

Raysha must find a path, forge her own techniques, and advance to the Initiate. But as the clock ticks down to the start of the contest, will she be able to prepare and overcome the fierce competition from the student elite? Can she control the vast ocean of aeon with sheer stubbornness and claim victory?

Provided the tournament is a straightforward content of will and power, Raysha is determined to find a way. Political incidents, internal and international, are something she'd like to stay in her past. 

*****

Or, to quote the amazing Andrew Rowe:

> "Scion of Storms is an fantastic sequel to one of my recent favorites and filled with all the best elements of progression fantasy -- epic battles, awesome techniques, and legendary monsters. We see the beginnings of Raysha's personal path in this book, and I can't wait to see where it leads in the future."

Or Silver Linings (who also has a release today, [check it out](https://www.amazon.com/Fabric-Reality-LitRPG-Adventure-Cases-ebook/dp/B0C91TP3CX)):

> "Like Soul Relic, Scion of Storms does an incredible job infusing every word of the narrative with life. Raysha's journey brings ever more challenges, but even more friends along with them - and the world remains just as deep and compelling, with yet more secrets waiting to be uncovered." - Silver Linings
