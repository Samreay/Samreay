## Post body

When it comes to stories I've always been interested in two things above all else: characters and philosophy.

I also love great action sequences but action alone won't carry something for me if the characters are flat or if there's no underlying theme beyond "i must get stronger."

Power systems are kind of the same way. I can appreciate an interesting system but without good characters as an anchor I'm going to lose interest pretty quickly.

I love progression fantasy. I'm a child of the 90s so I grew up on JRPGs, anime, sword and sorcery movies and a lot of 80s sci-fi. Progfantasy delivers pretty much exactly what I want out of entertainment.

Unfortunately the majority of the genre doesn't really do much more than that.

People view it as a popcorn genre, dismissively noting that most of the stories it contains are somewhere between fun but brainless and outright slop.

I wrote Wind and Water because I think progfantasy has the potential to deliver on everything people want from traditional fantasy or even literary works - but a lot of authors overfocus on the popcorn aspects (powers, fights, number go up etc)

Wind and Water is my first attempt at something that feels familiar while trying to elevate the kind of story the genre can tell.

There's plenty of progression, fights and memorable characters but as time goes on it asks questions about balance vs exploitation, the responsibility of those with power and what it really means to be a monster.

I don't expect that type of philosophical depth is going to be for everyone.

I also don't expect it to be wildly successful as it's my first foray into the genre.

But if you're someone who likes your prog fantasy with a little more substance or wants a more mature, kind natured MC it might be for you.

Also there's a bunch of weird animal companions doing weird and occasionally hilarious things. I love them very much.

Anyway, [here's the link.](https://www.royalroad.com/fiction/157898/wind-and-water-an-isekai-progression-fantasy-adventure)

Hope at least some of you have as much fun reading it as I'm having writing it.
