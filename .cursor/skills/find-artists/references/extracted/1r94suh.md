### Comment o69rcgb (parent t3_1r94suh)

Hi everyone!

I’m really excited to announce that **The Undying 2: The Reclamation**, a progression fantasy story with LitRPG elements, will be releasing next week.

This has been a passion project for me, and it turned out excellent — and based on feedback, even better than Book 1, which is hovering between a 4.8 and 4.9 on Amazon with stellar reviews. I’m incredibly grateful for that and proud of it, as it shows just how much I’ve improved since my first series, *Paths of Akashic*.

The **Audible** version for Book 2 is scheduled for early April, so for the audio enjoyers among you, it won’t be too long.

Narrated by Jonathan Waters & Amber Hartt. Art by Kartstudio.

Book 1’s cover has also been redone to match the new cover, though Audible, as usual, is taking its sweet time updating it.

As for what you can expect from the story, I feel this [post](https://www.reddit.com/r/litrpg/comments/1olras6/the_undying_1_the_warrior_republic_is_criminally/?utm_source=share&utm_medium=web3x&utm_name=web3xcss&utm_term=1&utm_content=share_button) explains the premise better than I could.

 

Blurb:

**The Sundering of the Blackened Sun draws near.**

After years of preparation, House Lysandor finally marches into the Voidlands to reclaim their ancestral birthright—the lost capital of Milaos. But the city has festered under undead dominion for decades, and the servants of the Void cannot allow the Republic to spread its wings.

Much is at stake as the servants of the Void push from all sides, the noose tightening around the Republic with each passing year. A failed expedition could mean the end of their family's ambitions and cast the Ferridan Republic into ruin.

For Asterion, this expedition holds an even greater promise—a chance to awaken the aspect of time. A power so rare few have ever wielded it, and even fewer survived long enough to master it. Should he succeed, he would finally claim his place among the Undying, gain the power necessary to uncover secrets buried in the ruins of fallen cities and lands long abandoned, and help drive the void's influence from their world.

The fate of Elystria teeters on a knife's edge. The primordials have set their pieces in motion, turning their gaze to Milaos—where the scales will tip toward salvation or annihilation.
